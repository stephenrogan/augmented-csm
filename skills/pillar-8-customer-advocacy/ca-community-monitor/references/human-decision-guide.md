# Human Decision Guide: Community Signal Interpretation

## Your Role

The Community Monitor tracks customer activity across community channels -- forums, user groups, social media mentions, review sites, community events, and peer networks. Your job is to decide which signals require a response, which represent opportunities, and which are noise you can safely ignore. The agent surfaces signals. You decide what they mean and whether they warrant action.

## When You Will See This

Community signals are surfaced:

1. **Real-time or near-real-time** -- monitoring community channels for mentions, sentiment shifts, and notable activity as they occur
2. **Weekly community health report** -- aggregated view of community sentiment, trending topics, active contributors, and emerging issues
3. **On request** -- when you need community engagement data for a specific account, or when preparing for a customer conversation where community activity is relevant context

## Decision Framework

### 1. Classify the signal

| Signal Type | Examples | Default Action |
|------------|---------|----------------|
| **Positive public mention** | G2 review, social media praise, community post recommending your product, conference mention | Acknowledge privately. Amplify publicly if appropriate. Log as advocacy signal for ca-advocacy-tracker |
| **Negative public mention** | Critical review, complaint thread, social media frustration, negative comparison | Assess validity first. Respond if public and actionable. Escalate internally if systemic |
| **Feature discussion** | Community debate about a capability, workaround sharing, "how do I do X" threads | Monitor for product feedback patterns. Engage if you can add genuine value. Feed to ic-feature-request-tracker if the request is substantive |
| **Competitive comparison** | Public comparison post, "thinking of switching" thread, alternative recommendation | Do not engage publicly. Feed intelligence to cm-competitive-response-prep. Public vendor responses in comparison threads almost always backfire |
| **Community leadership** | Customer actively helping other users, creating tutorials, moderating discussions, answering questions | Identify as advocacy candidate. Recognise and reward. These people are doing your job voluntarily |

### 2. Decide your response

| Decision | When to Use | Next Step |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Engage publicly** | Positive mention, helpful correction, or community question you can answer authentically | Respond as yourself (not as a brand account), add genuine value. Public engagement from a named CSM builds trust in ways that brand accounts cannot |
| **Engage privately** | Negative feedback that needs resolution, sensitive situation, or a conversation that would not benefit from an audience | Reach out directly to the customer. Resolve offline. Follow up publicly only if the customer is comfortable |
| **Escalate internally** | Pattern of negative sentiment, emerging issue affecting multiple customers, or a single post gaining traction that indicates a systemic problem | Brief your team with evidence. Propose a response strategy. Time matters -- a trending negative post that goes unanswered for 48 hours becomes the accepted narrative |
| **Monitor only** | General discussion, low-signal noise, or conversations that do not benefit from vendor participation | Log the signal. No action needed. Most community activity falls into this category and that is healthy |

## Special Handling

### Review site responses

Public responses to negative reviews are visible to every prospect evaluating your product. They are marketing assets disguised as customer service. Keep them professional, empathetic, and solution-oriented. Never argue. Never make excuses. Acknowledge the experience, address what you can, and invite a private conversation to resolve. One excellent response to a negative review can be more persuasive than ten positive reviews.

### Organic community champions

Customers who voluntarily help other users in community forums are worth more than any marketing campaign you can run. Identify them. Recognise them personally -- not just with badges or points, but with a direct message or call from their CSM. Protect the relationship. They are doing your job for free and their credibility with other customers exceeds yours because they have no incentive to be biased. Treat that with the respect it deserves.

### Viral negative posts

When a single negative post gains significant traction (shares, replies, media pickup), the standard response framework does not apply. This is a crisis communication situation. Escalate immediately to your manager and marketing/comms. Do not respond publicly without alignment on messaging. Speed matters but accuracy matters more. A fast wrong response is worse than a slightly delayed right one.
