# Advocacy Playbook

Best practices for each advocacy type.

## The Golden Rule

Advocacy is a withdrawal from the relationship bank account. Every ask costs goodwill. Only ask when the balance is high, and always deposit more than you withdraw. The CSM's job is to ensure the customer gets disproportionate value from the relationship before any advocacy ask is made.

## Reference Calls

**When to ask:** After a successful QBR where the customer expressed satisfaction, after a major milestone achieved, or when the customer voluntarily praises the product.

**How to ask:** "Would you be open to a brief call with a company evaluating us? They are in a similar situation to yours and I think your perspective would be really valuable to them." Frame it as the customer being the expert, not as doing you a favour.

**Preparation:** Brief the advocate on what the prospect cares about. Brief the prospect on the advocate's story. Make it easy for the advocate -- they should not need to prepare extensively.

**Follow-up:** Thank the advocate personally. Share the outcome if appropriate ("they signed -- your conversation was a big factor"). This reinforces the value of their contribution.

**Frequency limit:** No more than once per quarter per advocate.

## Case Studies

**When to ask:** When pa-value-reporter shows strong, quantifiable outcomes. The best case studies have specific numbers.

**How to ask:** "Your results have been exceptional -- a 40% reduction in manual reporting time. Would you be open to us documenting your story? We would handle the writing, and you would have full approval over the final version."

**Process:** CSM coordinates the interview. Marketing or content team writes. Customer reviews and approves. Legal reviews if required by customer's company policy.

**Timeline:** Expect 4-8 weeks from interview to published case study. The bottleneck is usually customer approval, not content creation.

**Critical:** Never publish without explicit written approval. Never exaggerate metrics. Never attribute quotes the customer did not actually say.

## Review Sites (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius)

**When to ask:** After a positive NPS response, after a successful support resolution, or after a milestone achievement. Timing matters -- ask when goodwill is highest.

**How to ask:** Keep it simple. "Would you mind leaving a quick review on G2? It takes about 5 minutes and it helps other companies like yours find us."

**Do not:** Offer incentives that violate review platform policies. Script the review. Ask customers who are not genuinely satisfied -- negative reviews from reluctant advocates are worse than no review.

## NPS Follow-Up Framework

**Promoters (9-10):** Thank them. Ask what specifically they value most (this is case study and testimonial material). Assess advocacy readiness. If appropriate, make a soft advocacy ask.

**Passives (7-8):** Thank them. Ask what would make them a 9 or 10. This is product feedback and adoption opportunity, not advocacy territory.

**Detractors (0-6):** Do not ask for advocacy. Focus entirely on understanding and addressing the issue. Route to bi-risk-detector and the CSM's risk triage process.
