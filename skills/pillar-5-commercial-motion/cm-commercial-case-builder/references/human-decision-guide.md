# Human Decision Guide: Commercial Case Review

## Your Role

The Commercial Case Builder assembles the data, benchmarks, and financial framing for expansion and upsell conversations. Your job is to evaluate whether the case is ready to present, adjust the narrative for the specific buyer, and decide on timing. The agent builds the ammunition. You choose the moment and the angle.

## When You Will See This

Commercial case materials are generated when:

1. **bi-expansion-detector flags a signal** and you acknowledge it warrants a commercial conversation
2. **lo-renewal-manager** flags a renewal that includes an expansion component
3. **You request materials** for an expansion conversation you are planning proactively

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess case strength

- Is the value evidence concrete or inferred? Concrete = usage data, ROI metrics, customer-stated outcomes. Inferred = assumptions based on segment benchmarks. Concrete evidence closes deals. Inferred evidence starts conversations.
- Does the financial model hold up? Check the assumptions behind projected ROI, payback period, and cost comparison. If you would not defend the numbers in front of the customer's CFO, the case is not ready.
- Is there a compelling trigger event? Budget cycle, contract milestone, org change, or new initiative that creates urgency. Without a trigger, even a strong case sits in a queue.

### 2. Evaluate buyer readiness

- Has the champion signalled willingness to discuss expansion?
- Is budget authority identified? A strong case presented to the wrong person is wasted effort.
- Are there blockers the case doesn't address? Competing priorities, internal politics, prior negative experiences with upsell attempts, or unresolved support issues.

### 3. Decide on action

| Decision | When to Use | Next Step |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Present now** | Case is strong, timing is right, buyer is engaged | Schedule the conversation. Tailor the narrative to the specific stakeholder's priorities |
| **Strengthen first** | Data gaps, weak ROI evidence, or missing trigger event | Identify what's needed -- usage milestone, customer quote, benchmark data -- and set a timeline to revisit |
| **Hold** | Buyer is not ready, timing is wrong, or relationship needs attention first | Log the opportunity, set a review date, continue value delivery without commercial pressure |
| **Redirect** | Expansion is wrong play -- customer needs stabilisation or has unresolved issues | Address underlying issues first. Presenting an expansion case to a frustrated customer accelerates churn, not growth |

## Special Handling

### Multi-stakeholder deals

When the expansion involves multiple budget holders or departments, build separate value narratives for each. The CFO cares about cost efficiency and payback. The VP cares about team productivity and strategic alignment. The end users care about capability gaps and workflow improvement. One case, multiple framings -- the agent provides the data, you choose the lens for each audience.

### Competitive displacement risk

If the customer is evaluating alternatives, do not lead with expansion. Lead with differentiation and value reinforcement. Expansion conversations during competitive evaluations feel tone-deaf and accelerate the evaluation rather than slowing it. Secure the base before building on it.

### The "not yet" response

When a customer says "not now" to expansion, this is not a rejection -- it is a timing signal. Ask: "What would need to be true for this to make sense?" Their answer gives you the trigger condition for the next attempt. Log it. Set the review date. Return when conditions are met.
