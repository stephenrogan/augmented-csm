# Human Decision Guide: Stakeholder Change Response

## Your Role

The Stakeholder Change Detector identifies when key contacts leave, join, change roles, or shift influence within your accounts. Your job is to assess the impact of each change on the relationship and decide how to respond. The agent detects the change. You decide what it means and what to do about it.

## When You Will See This

Stakeholder change alerts are triggered:

1. **CRM contact update** -- when a contact record changes (new contact added, existing contact marked inactive, role change)
2. **Engagement pattern shift** -- when a previously active contact suddenly goes dark, or a new contact appears in meeting attendance or support interactions
3. **Weekly external scan** -- LinkedIn monitoring (if integrated) detects role changes, departures, or new hires among tracked contacts

## Decision Framework

### 1. Classify the change

| Change Type | Risk Level | Response Window |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Champion departure** | Critical | 24-48 hours. This is the highest-urgency stakeholder event |
| **Executive sponsor change** | High | Within the week. New exec needs onboarding to your value story |
| **New decision-maker added** | Medium-High (opportunity or threat) | Within two weeks. Research before engaging |
| **Power user departure** | Medium | Within the week. Assess adoption impact and identify replacement |
| **Reorganisation affecting your buyers** | Variable | Assess within 48 hours, act within two weeks. Reorg signals can be positive or negative |
| **Contact role change (lateral or promotion)** | Low-Medium | Within the month. Updated stakeholder map, adjusted engagement approach |

### 2. Assess impact

- How dependent was the relationship on this person? Single-threaded relationships are existentially vulnerable to any contact change. Multi-threaded relationships absorb departures more gracefully.
- Who remains? Map the surviving stakeholders and their disposition toward your product. If the remaining contacts are neutral or negative, you have a relationship rebuild ahead.
- Is the replacement known? If yes, research them immediately -- their background, their likely priorities, their previous experience with your product or competitors. If no, your remaining contacts are your bridge to the replacement.

### 3. Decide response

| Decision | When to Use | Next Step |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Immediate outreach** | Champion or sponsor departed, relationship is single-threaded or thinly covered | Contact remaining stakeholders within 24 hours. Request introduction to replacement. Brief your manager on the risk |
| **Relationship rebuild** | Key contact replaced by someone with no history with you | Treat as a new onboarding -- introductory meeting, value recap, establish cadence. Do not assume inherited goodwill |
| **Opportunity pursuit** | New stakeholder is senior, comes from a company that used your product, or has a mandate aligned with your value prop | Research and engage proactively with a tailored approach. New decision-makers often bring new budgets and new priorities |
| **Monitor** | Change is in a non-critical role or the account is deeply multi-threaded with strong remaining coverage | Log the change, update the stakeholder map, review at next touchpoint |

## Special Handling

### The departed champion who moves to a new company

This is a pipeline opportunity disguised as a loss. Your best advocates are customers who already believe in your product. Track where they go. When appropriate, reach out -- not as a sales motion, but as a continuation of the relationship. Time it right: give them 60-90 days to settle into the new role before proposing a conversation about their new environment. A warm introduction to your sales team from a departed champion is among the highest-conversion pipeline sources.

### The hostile replacement

Occasionally a departing champion is replaced by someone who preferred a competitor at their previous company, or who has a mandate to rationalise vendor spend. Recognise this quickly. Do not assume neutral intent. Lead with value evidence and executive alignment. If the replacement is evaluating alternatives, you are in a competitive response situation -- hand off to cm-competitive-response-prep.

### Reorg signals

When multiple contacts change roles simultaneously, or when the org chart shifts around your buyer, something structural is happening. Do not react to individual changes in isolation. Wait 48 hours for the picture to clarify, then map the new structure and assess whether your coverage still holds.
