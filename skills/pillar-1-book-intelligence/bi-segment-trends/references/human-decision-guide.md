# Human Decision Guide: Segment Trend Interpretation

## Your Role

The Segment Trends Agent identifies patterns across cohorts of accounts -- by tier, size, industry, or contract age. Your job is to decide whether a trend is actionable, who needs to know, and what intervention (if any) it warrants at the portfolio or programme level. Individual account work is handled by other skills. This is about the bigger picture.

## When You Will See This

Segment trend reports surface under three conditions:

1. **Scheduled** -- monthly or quarterly segment analysis
2. **Triggered** -- a segment metric deviated significantly from baseline (e.g., mid-market churn rate spiked 2x)
3. **On-demand** -- you or leadership requested a segment-level view

## How to Read a Segment Trend

### 1. Validate the signal

Is the sample size large enough to be meaningful? A trend across 50 accounts is more reliable than one across 8. Check whether a single large account is skewing the segment average.

### 2. Distinguish trend from noise

- One bad month in a segment is noise. Two consecutive months trending the same direction is a signal.
- Seasonal patterns (Q4 budget freezes, summer slowdowns) can create false trends. Compare year-over-year, not just month-over-month.
- New product releases or pricing changes can temporarily distort segment behaviour. Account for these before concluding a trend is organic.

### 3. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Emerging risk in a segment** | Health or usage declining across a cohort, not just individual accounts | Investigate root cause. Is it product-related, market-related, or service-related? Brief CS leadership with evidence |
| **Expansion opportunity pattern** | A segment shows consistent growth signals (usage ceiling, feature-gating) | Build a segment-level expansion play. Brief sales and CS leadership |
| **Churn cluster** | Multiple accounts in the same segment churning with similar root causes | This is a systemic issue, not an account issue. Escalate to CS leadership and product with the pattern |
| **Segment outperforming** | A cohort consistently scores above peers on health, adoption, or NRR | Identify what is different about this segment. Extract the playbook for underperforming segments |
| **Inconclusive data** | Trend is visible but cause is unclear or sample size is small | Monitor for another period before acting. Set a review date |

## Special Handling

### Product-Correlated Segment Trends

If a segment trend correlates with a product change (release, deprecation, pricing), route to the CS-product sync with evidence. This is not a CS problem to solve alone.

### Cross-Segment Divergence

When one segment improves while another declines on the same metric, the cause is almost certainly segment-specific (not platform-wide). Investigate what differs: onboarding approach, CSM coverage model, product fit, or competitive pressure.

### Leadership Reporting

Segment trends are the primary input for strategic resource allocation. When presenting to leadership, lead with the business impact (ARR at risk, NRR trajectory) and the recommended intervention, not the raw data. Leadership decides resource allocation; you provide the evidence and recommendation.
