# Human Decision Guide: Competitive Signals

## Core Principle

Never lead a competitive conversation with defensiveness. The customer is evaluating alternatives because they have an unmet need. Your job is to find and address the unmet need.

## Response by Signal Strength

### High-Strength (Direct Evaluative Mention)
1. Investigate the root cause -- what need is unmet?
2. Prepare your response around the need, not the competitor
3. Acknowledge naturally if the mention was in a direct conversation
4. Brief your manager with: evidence, hypothesis, plan, and what you need

### Medium-Strength (Behavioural or Indirect)
1. Note the signal and look for corroborating evidence
2. In your next natural touchpoint, explore whether there are unmet needs
3. If the signal persists or clusters, escalate to more direct investigation

### Low-Strength (Engagement Decline Only)
1. Treat as a general risk signal through normal triage
2. Keep the competitive hypothesis in mind but do not lead with it
