# Human Decision Guide: Risk Signal Triage

## Your Role

The Risk Signal Detector surfaces accounts that need human attention. Your job is to triage: decide what to do with each alert. The agent has identified signals and ranked them by severity. You bring the relationship context, strategic judgment, and intervention skill.

## Triage Framework

For each alert in your risk queue, work through this sequence:

### 1. Assess signal quality

- Is this a real signal or noise? Check for data issues, known seasonal patterns, or one-off events
- Are multiple signals clustering? Clustered signals are far more serious than isolated ones
- Is this new information or something you already know about?

### 2. Check what you know that the data does not

- Have you had a recent conversation that explains this signal?
- Is there an internal factor at the customer (reorg, budget freeze, leadership change) that accounts for the data?
- Does the signal contradict your relationship read? If so, which do you trust more -- and why?

### 3. Decide on action

| Triage Decision | When to Use | Next Step |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Investigate** | Signal is credible but cause is unclear | Pull full account brief, review recent interactions, then decide on outreach |
| **Act now** | Signal is credible and cause is understood -- intervention needed | Choose the appropriate intervention: call, executive engagement, save play, escalation |
| **Monitor** | Signal is minor, isolated, or has a known benign cause | Set a review date (7-14 days). If signal persists or worsens, escalate to Investigate |
| **Dismiss** | Signal is noise (data issue, seasonal, already resolved) | Mark as dismissed with reason. Your dismissal reason improves the system |

### 4. Log your decision

Every triage decision should be logged. This creates the feedback loop:
- Dismissed alerts with rationale help calibrate severity weights
- Investigation outcomes help identify which signals correlate with real churn risk
- Intervention outcomes help measure the ROI of early detection

## Special Handling

### Champion Departure

Do not wait for more data. This is the highest-impact signal. Your immediate priorities:
1. Identify the new primary contact or sponsor
2. Reach out to the remaining contacts to understand the transition
3. Brief your manager if the account is strategic
4. Update the stakeholder map

### Competitive Displacement

Do not lead with "I heard you're evaluating competitors." Instead:
1. Review the evidence -- is it a direct mention or a behavioural inference?
2. If direct mention: acknowledge it naturally in your next conversation. "I noticed your team had some questions about [capability area] -- I wanted to make sure we're covering that well for you"
3. If behavioural inference: investigate further before acting. Increased data export activity could mean many things
4. Brief your manager. Competitive displacement often requires a strategic response beyond the CSM level

### Clustered Signals (3+)

Three or more signals converging on one account is the strongest predictor of near-term churn. Treat this as urgent regardless of individual signal severity:
1. Pull the full account brief immediately
2. Review all signals together -- is there a common root cause?
3. Decide whether this requires a proactive outreach, an internal escalation, or both
4. If the account is within 90 days of renewal, brief your manager today
