# Human Decision Guide: Account Brief Review

## Your Role

The Account Brief Agent assembles a comprehensive data snapshot of an account from all connected systems. Your job is to interpret the brief, decide what matters, and determine what action (if any) it warrants. The agent compiles facts. You provide the "so what."

## When You Will See This

Account briefs are generated in three contexts:

1. **Pre-meeting preparation** -- before a QBR, renewal, or escalation call
2. **On-demand** -- you requested context on a specific account
3. **Triggered** -- another skill (renewal manager, escalation router) needed a full account picture

## How to Use the Brief

The brief is a data assembly, not a recommendation. Work through it in this order:

### 1. Check for surprises

Scan the brief for anything that contradicts your current understanding of the account. Has health changed since you last looked? Are there stakeholder gaps you did not know about? Is usage trending differently than expected?

### 2. Identify what is missing

The brief compiles system data. It cannot include:
- Your last informal conversation with the champion
- Internal politics at the customer
- Strategic context the customer shared in confidence
- Anything that happened outside tracked channels

Add your context mentally before drawing conclusions.

### 3. Decide what to act on

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Brief confirms your read** | Data matches your understanding of the account | Use the brief as evidence in your meeting or conversation. No additional investigation needed |
| **Brief reveals a gap** | Data shows something you did not know (stakeholder change, usage shift, support spike) | Investigate the gap before your meeting. Do not walk in uninformed |
| **Brief contradicts your read** | Data says one thing, your relationship context says another | Trust your read but investigate the data point. You may be right, or you may be missing something |
| **Brief is stale** | Key data points are outdated or missing | Flag to CS Ops if systemic. If isolated, pull fresh data manually for the critical fields |
| **Brief is for an unfamiliar account** | Covering for another CSM or inheriting an account | Read every section carefully. This is your primary context source. Follow up with the previous CSM for qualitative colour |

## Special Handling

### Pre-Renewal Brief

Focus on: health trajectory, stakeholder coverage (is the economic buyer engaged?), open risk signals, and contract history. The brief tells you the data story. You decide the renewal strategy.

### Pre-Escalation Brief

Focus on: timeline of the issue, resolution attempts already made, and customer communication status. The receiving team needs this context to avoid re-trying failed approaches. Add your read on customer patience and urgency.

### Account Handoff Brief

When inheriting an account, the brief is your starting point -- not your complete picture. Schedule a call with the previous CSM within the first week. The brief cannot capture relationship dynamics, informal commitments, or personality nuances.
