# Brief Templates Reference

Template variations for different consumption contexts. The Account Briefing Generator adapts its output based on the specified context.

## Pre-Call Brief

**Purpose**: Give the CSM everything they need to walk into a call prepared.

**Emphasis**: Recent activity, open items, stakeholder context, conversation history.

**Structure**:
1. Header block (account name, ARR, health, renewal date)
2. Last interaction summary (what was discussed, what was committed)
3. Open items (support tickets, pending actions, unresolved questions)
4. Stakeholder context (who is on the call, their role, last engagement)
5. Attention flags (anything the CSM should raise or be prepared for)

**What to exclude**: Full health component breakdown, segment benchmarking, expansion signals (unless the call purpose is commercial). Keep it tight -- the CSM is reading this 5 minutes before the call.

## QBR Preparation Brief

**Purpose**: Give the CSM the data and narrative arc for a Quarterly Business Review.

**Emphasis**: Value delivered, usage trends, health trajectory, strategic recommendations.

**Structure**:
1. Header block
2. Health trajectory (this quarter vs. last quarter, component-level changes)
3. Usage metrics with benchmark comparison (show the customer where they stand vs. peers)
4. Value delivered summary (key workflow completions, time saved, outcomes achieved)
5. Adoption gaps (features available but unused, use cases not yet explored)
6. Open items and recent support history
7. Strategic recommendations (what the CSM should propose in the QBR)

**What to exclude**: Internal risk signals (do not share risk scoring with the customer). Keep competitive intelligence internal.

## Renewal Preparation Brief

**Purpose**: Give the CSM and their manager the full picture ahead of a renewal negotiation.

**Emphasis**: Commercial data, health trajectory, risk signals, stakeholder map, negotiation context.

**Structure**:
1. Header block with commercial detail (ARR, contract terms, pricing tier, last expansion date)
2. Health trajectory over the full contract period
3. Value delivered (ROI data, usage growth, outcomes)
4. Risk signals (anything that could affect the renewal conversation)
5. Expansion signals (if the renewal is also an expansion opportunity)
6. Stakeholder map with economic buyer and decision-maker coverage
7. Competitive intelligence (if any signals are active)
8. Recommended approach (renew as-is, renew with expansion, renew with concession, escalate)

## Escalation Brief

**Purpose**: Build the evidence chain for an internal escalation to CS leadership, product, or engineering.

**Emphasis**: Timeline, impact, support history, customer sentiment.

**Structure**:
1. Header block
2. Escalation summary (what is being escalated and why)
3. Timeline of deterioration (when the issue started, how it has progressed)
4. Impact on the customer (usage changes, sentiment changes, at-risk revenue)
5. Support history (relevant tickets, resolution attempts, days open)
6. Customer communications (what the customer has been told, what they expect)
7. Requested action (what the escalation is asking for)

## Executive Summary Brief

**Purpose**: Brief a VP or C-level executive on an account in under 30 seconds.

**Emphasis**: Headline numbers, key risk or opportunity, recommended action.

**Structure**:
1. One-line summary: "[Account] is [health status] with [ARR] and [days] to renewal. [Key issue or opportunity in one sentence]."
2. Health score with trend
3. Top risk or opportunity signal (one only -- the most important)
4. Recommended executive action (if any)

**Length**: 4-5 lines maximum. If the executive wants detail, they ask for the full brief.
