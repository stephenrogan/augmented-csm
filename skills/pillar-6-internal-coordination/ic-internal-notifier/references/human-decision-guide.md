# Human Decision Guide: Internal Notification Review

## Your Role

The Internal Notifier generates and routes notifications to internal teams when account events require cross-functional awareness -- risk alerts, escalations, milestone completions, renewal changes, stakeholder movements. Your job is to decide which notifications warrant your personal follow-up beyond the automated alert, and to prevent the notification system from becoming noise.

## When You Will See This

Notifications are generated:

1. **Event-driven** -- fires when a defined trigger condition is met from any pillar skill (risk alert, escalation, stakeholder change, milestone completion)
2. **Daily digest** -- aggregated summary of account status changes for configured recipients
3. **On request** -- when you manually trigger a notification to a specific internal audience

## Decision Framework

### 1. Scan for noise vs. signal

Most notifications are informational and need no action from you. Focus your attention on:
- Notifications involving your top-tier accounts by revenue or strategic importance
- Notifications that combine with other recent signals (clustering indicates a pattern worth investigating)
- Notifications sent to leadership or cross-functional partners that may generate questions back to you

### 2. Decide your response

| Decision | When to Use | Next Step |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **No action** | Notification is purely informational, system is working as designed | Let the automated notification do its job. Your time is better spent elsewhere |
| **Add context** | The notification is accurate but lacks relationship context that changes interpretation | Reply to the thread or message the recipient with your context before they act on incomplete information |
| **Pre-empt** | The notification will reach leadership or a cross-functional partner who will have questions | Brief them directly before or alongside the notification. Being ahead of the information flow protects your credibility |
| **Suppress** | The notification would cause unnecessary alarm, is based on stale data, or is a false positive | Mark as reviewed, add suppression reason, update the underlying data if needed. Document why you suppressed it |
| **Escalate** | The notification reveals something more serious than the automated classification suggests | Add your assessment and route to the appropriate person with urgency context |

## Special Handling

### Leadership-bound notifications

Any notification routed to your VP or above should be reviewed by you first. Not to filter it -- transparency matters -- but to ensure you can answer the questions it will generate. Being caught unaware by your own team's notification system is a credibility hit that takes weeks to recover from. If the notification contains bad news, brief your manager before it lands.

### Notification fatigue

If you are reviewing more than 10 notifications per day, the thresholds are too sensitive. Adjust the trigger rules rather than habituating to noise. The moment notifications become background noise, the system has failed its core purpose -- which is to surface the things that matter. Calibrate aggressively. A notification system that sends 5 high-signal alerts per week is worth more than one that sends 10 per day.

### Digest vs. real-time

Not everything needs to be real-time. Risk alerts and escalations: real-time. Milestone completions and routine status changes: daily digest. If the recipient would not act on the information within an hour, it belongs in the digest, not the real-time stream.
