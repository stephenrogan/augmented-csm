# Human Decision Guide: Enablement Planning

## Your Role

The Enablement Orchestrator coordinates training logistics -- scheduling sessions, matching needs to resources, tracking completion. Your job is to decide what enablement the customer actually needs, how to position it, and whether the root cause is knowledge (enablement can fix) or something else entirely. The agent manages the programme. You diagnose the need and deliver the value.

## When You Will See This

Enablement recommendations surface when:

1. **Adoption gap with knowledge root cause** -- a feature is not used because the customer does not know how
2. **New customer onboarding** -- foundational training is needed
3. **Personnel change** -- a trained user left and their replacement needs onboarding
4. **Product release** -- new capabilities that relevant customers should learn about

## Decision Framework

### 1. Validate the root cause

Before approving enablement:
- Is this actually a knowledge gap? If the customer tried the feature and stopped, the issue may be product friction, not training
- Is the customer ready to learn? If they are overwhelmed with an ongoing project or understaffed, training will not stick
- Is the right audience identified? Training the wrong people wastes everyone's time

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Approve enablement** | Knowledge gap is confirmed, customer is ready, right audience identified | Proceed with the recommended resource type. Adjust format if needed (live vs. self-serve) |
| **Adjust format** | Recommended approach does not fit this customer | Swap live training for self-serve (busy team) or vice versa (complex feature, hands-on learners) |
| **Defer** | Customer is not ready (too busy, mid-project, understaffed) | Set a reminder for when the timing improves. Do not push training they cannot absorb |
| **Investigate further** | Not sure whether the issue is knowledge, motivation, or product | Talk to the customer before prescribing training. Ask about their experience with the feature |
| **Escalate to product** | Enablement was delivered but adoption did not improve | The issue is not knowledge -- it is the product. Route to pa-feedback-aggregator with the evidence |

## Special Handling

### Positioning Enablement

"Training" can sound condescending. Frame enablement as "helping your team get more value from capabilities you are already paying for." The goal is outcome-driven, not curriculum-driven. The customer should feel empowered, not remediated.

### Post-Enablement Follow-Up

Training delivered is not the same as training absorbed. Check adoption 30 days after enablement. If the feature is still not being used, the training did not land -- either the format was wrong, the content was insufficient, or the root cause was not knowledge after all.

### Personnel Changes

When a key user leaves, their replacement needs enablement urgently. Institutional knowledge walks out the door. Do not wait for the new person to ask -- proactively offer a compressed onboarding tailored to the departing user's workflows.
