# Human Decision Guide: Value Evidence Review

## Your Role

The Value Reporter computes value metrics -- time saved, adoption ROI, custom KPI progress, and time-to-value benchmarks. Your job is to decide which metrics to present, validate the methodology, and ensure the numbers are credible enough to share with the customer. The agent quantifies. You validate and narrate.

## When You Will See This

Value evidence is generated:

1. **QBR preparation** -- value slides for cc-qbr-deck-builder
2. **Renewal preparation** -- value narrative for lo-renewal-manager
3. **Expansion conversation** -- value evidence for cm-commercial-case-builder
4. **Monthly snapshot** -- updated value metrics across your portfolio

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess credibility

Before presenting value metrics to a customer:
- Is the methodology defensible? "Hours saved" estimates use assumptions the customer may challenge. Know what the assumptions are and whether the customer will accept them
- Has the customer validated any of these numbers? Customer-confirmed metrics are gold. Agent-estimated metrics are silver. Unverified claims are bronze at best
- Could the customer produce a different number? If their internal data would contradict your claim, do not present it

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Present with confidence** | Metric is based on direct measurement or customer-confirmed data | Lead with it. This is your strongest retention and expansion evidence |
| **Present with caveats** | Metric is estimated, methodology is reasonable but unverified | Share the metric with the methodology visible. "Based on X assumption, we estimate Y." Transparency builds trust |
| **Use internally only** | Metric is too estimated or speculative for customer consumption | Use it for your own prioritisation and strategy. Do not present externally |
| **Invest in custom KPIs** | Universal metrics do not capture what this customer actually cares about | Work with the customer to define custom success metrics. This investment pays off at every subsequent QBR and renewal |
| **Challenge the metric** | Agent-computed value seems too high or too low based on your knowledge | Investigate before presenting. A credible conservative number is more powerful than an impressive number the customer will dispute |

## Special Handling

### The ROI Conversation

ROI is the most powerful and most dangerous value metric. If credible, it is the single best retention argument. If challenged, it undermines everything. Never present ROI you cannot defend. When in doubt, present outcomes ("your team saved 340 hours") rather than ROI ("you achieved 3.2x return").

### Custom KPIs vs. Universal Metrics

Custom KPIs (the metrics the customer defined as their success criteria) are always more compelling than universal metrics. If no custom KPIs exist, use the next QBR to establish them with the customer. The investment in defining baselines pays dividends at every subsequent review.

### Value Plateau

If value metrics are flat quarter-over-quarter, the customer may be extracting all available value from their current adoption level. This is a signal for either adoption deepening (more features) or expansion (more users/capabilities). Frame the plateau as "you have maximised value at this level -- here is what the next level unlocks."
