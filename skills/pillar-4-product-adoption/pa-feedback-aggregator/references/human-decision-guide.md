# Human Decision Guide: Feedback Theme Validation

## Your Role

The Feedback Aggregator collects product feedback from support tickets, calls, surveys, and notes, then clusters it into weighted themes. Your job is to validate the clustering, assess whether the weighting reflects business reality, and decide how to present the findings to the product team. The agent aggregates and themes. You advocate and prioritise.

## When You Will See This

Feedback reports surface:

1. **Monthly** -- aggregated feedback report with ranked themes for the CS-product sync
2. **On-demand** -- per-account feedback view for QBR or renewal preparation
3. **Triggered** -- a new theme emerges with enough breadth to warrant attention

## Decision Framework

### 1. Validate the clustering

Before presenting to the product team:
- Did the agent merge distinct issues into one theme? "Reporting" could cover five separate problems. Split if needed
- Did the agent split related issues that are really one theme? "Dashboard speed" and "report load time" may be the same root cause
- Is the representative feedback genuinely representative? One dramatic quote may not reflect the broader theme

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Present to product** | Theme is validated, breadth is real, impact is significant | Bring to the CS-product sync with the evidence package. Lead with the business impact, not just the request count |
| **Reweight** | Composite score does not reflect the real business urgency | Override the scoring. A theme with low breadth but one account threatening to churn may deserve top priority |
| **Investigate further** | Theme looks real but evidence is thin or contradictory | Dig into the raw feedback before presenting. Weak evidence weakens your advocacy |
| **Deprioritise** | Theme is vocal but narrow (1-2 loud accounts, not a portfolio pattern) | Note it but do not lead with it. Product teams correctly discount single-customer noise |
| **Communicate to customers** | A theme has been addressed (feature shipped or workaround available) | Proactively inform the customers who raised the feedback. Closing the loop builds trust |

## Special Handling

### Churn-Correlated Themes

Feedback themes that appear in churned account post-mortems carry outsized weight. Present the churn correlation prominently, but frame as "correlated with" not "caused by." Product teams (correctly) scrutinise causal claims.

### The Vocal Minority Problem

Some customers raise feedback loudly and frequently. After deduplication, their voice counts once. But if they are your most strategic account, their feedback may legitimately deserve more weight than a simple count suggests. Use judgment.

### Product Team Relationships

How you present feedback matters as much as what you present. Come with data, not complaints. Come with impact, not anecdotes. Come with suggested priority, not demands. The CS-product relationship is a long-term asset -- invest in credibility by being rigorous with your evidence.
