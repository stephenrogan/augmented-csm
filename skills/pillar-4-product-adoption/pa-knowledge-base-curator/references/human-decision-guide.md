# Human Decision Guide: Knowledge Base Gap Triage

## Your Role

The Knowledge Base Curator identifies content gaps from support patterns, detects stale articles after product releases, and recommends resources to customers based on adoption profiles. Your job is to prioritise which gaps to fix, decide what to share with customers, and ensure the help content stays trustworthy. The agent audits the content. You prioritise and execute.

## When You Will See This

Content alerts surface when:

1. **Gap detected** -- recurring support questions with no matching documentation
2. **Stale article flagged** -- a product release invalidated an existing article
3. **Customer resource recommendation** -- personalised content suggestions for a specific account
4. **Weekly content health report** -- summary of gaps, staleness, and deflection metrics

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess gap priority

Not all content gaps deserve immediate attention. Before acting:
- How many tickets is this gap generating? 10+ tickets per month is urgent. 2-3 is a backlog item
- What is the ARR of affected accounts? Gaps affecting enterprise accounts carry more weight
- How complex is the content to create? A simple FAQ takes 30 minutes. A comprehensive tutorial takes a day

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Create content now** | High-traffic gap, clear pattern, straightforward to document | Route to the content team with the gap specification. Include ticket examples as evidence |
| **Update stale article** | Article is outdated but still heavily trafficked | Prioritise by traffic volume and severity of outdated information. A misleading article is worse than a missing one |
| **Share resources with customer** | Adoption gap has matching help content the customer has not seen | Include the resource in your next touchpoint. Frame it as value, not homework |
| **Backlog** | Gap is real but low volume or low complexity impact | Log it for the content team's backlog. Revisit in 30 days to check if volume has increased |
| **Address through CSM coaching** | Gap is too nuanced for a help article -- requires relationship context | Handle it directly in your customer interactions. Not every knowledge gap has a documentation solution |

## Special Handling

### Breaking Articles

An article that actively misleads readers is worse than no article at all. After a product release, prioritise "breaking" articles (content that references removed or fundamentally changed features) above all other content work. A customer who follows outdated instructions and fails will blame the product, not the documentation.

### Resource Recommendations

When sharing help content with customers, context matters. Sending a help article to a frustrated customer feels dismissive. Sending it to a curious customer feels helpful. Know the difference before including a resource link in your touchpoint.

### The Deflection Metric

Track whether new documentation actually reduces ticket volume on that topic. If you create content and tickets persist, the content is not solving the problem -- either the article is insufficient, the issue is more complex than documentation can address, or customers prefer human support. Adjust accordingly.
