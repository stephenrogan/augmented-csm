# Human Decision Guide: Internal Brief Review

## Your Role

The Internal Brief Writer assembles data into structured documents for internal audiences -- escalation briefs, leadership summaries, cross-functional context documents. Your job is to add the judgment layer: the "so what," the recommendation, and the political awareness that transforms data into action. The agent builds the evidence. You tell the story.

## When You Will See This

Internal briefs are generated:

1. **Escalation triggered** -- an escalation needs a structured brief for the receiving team
2. **Leadership review** -- a summary is needed for a manager review, pipeline meeting, or executive briefing
3. **Cross-functional request** -- another team needs context on an account
4. **On-demand** -- you need an internal document for a specific purpose

## Decision Framework

### 1. Review for audience fit

Before distributing:
- Does the detail level match the audience? VPs want 5 lines. Escalation teams need 2 pages
- Is anything included that should not be shared with this audience? Revenue data to engineering, internal relationship assessments to product
- Is the "specific ask" genuinely specific? "Please help" fails. A clear action with a deadline succeeds

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Add framing and send** | Data assembly is correct, needs your narrative layer | Write the "so what" section and recommendation. Add urgency calibration based on your relationship read |
| **Adjust for audience** | Brief is too detailed or too light for the specific recipients | Restructure. Know your audience -- some leaders want data, others want the headline and recommendation |
| **Add sensitivity notes** | Brief contains information that requires careful handling | Mark what can be shared broadly and what should stay within a restricted audience |
| **Reframe** | The data is correct but the positioning needs to change given internal dynamics | Adjust tone and emphasis based on how the receiving team will respond. Knowing who is under pressure changes how you frame a request |
| **Hold for context** | You need more information before the brief can be actionable | Gather the missing context before distributing an incomplete brief. Sending prematurely wastes the audience's attention |

## Special Handling

### Escalation Briefs

The escalation brief is your tool for getting internal action. Its quality directly determines how quickly you get help. Include the full timeline of what has been tried and failed -- this prevents the receiving team from wasting time re-attempting failed approaches. Always include the customer communication status so the receiving team knows what the customer expects.

### Political Awareness

The brief assembles facts. You navigate internal politics. Know which teams are receptive, which are under resource pressure, which leaders respond to data versus relationship arguments. Frame accordingly.

### The Recommendation Gap

A brief without a recommendation is a report. The most important content you add is: "Here is what I recommend and why." Even if the audience disagrees, a clear recommendation shows you have thought through the problem and accelerates the decision.
