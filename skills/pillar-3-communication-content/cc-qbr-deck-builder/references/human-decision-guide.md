# Human Decision Guide: QBR Deck Review

## Your Role

The QBR Deck Builder populates slides with data -- usage metrics, health trends, support history, value evidence, and benchmarks. Your job is to turn the data deck into a strategic conversation: deciding what story to tell, which data to emphasise, writing the recommendation slides, and preparing for the discussion. The agent fills the slides. You own the narrative.

## When You Will See This

Decks are generated at T-7 before a scheduled QBR. You review and customise before the meeting.

## Decision Framework

### 1. Review the data

Before adding narrative:
- Is the data fresh? Anything stale undermines the deck's credibility. Flag stale sources immediately
- Are the numbers accurate? Spot-check 2-3 key figures against your own knowledge of the account
- Is anything missing? Data the customer will expect to see (a metric they track, a project update) that the deck did not include

### 2. Decide on approach

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Customise and present** | Data is strong, story is clear | Write the strategic recommendation slides. Cut slides the customer will not care about. Prepare discussion questions |
| **Reframe the narrative** | Data tells a different story than what you planned to present | Adjust emphasis. The same data can frame "strong performance" or "room for improvement" depending on your objective for the meeting |
| **Address gaps proactively** | Data reveals a weakness the customer will notice | Do not hide it. Present it with context and a plan. Customers trust CSMs who acknowledge challenges, not those who cherry-pick data |
| **Simplify** | Deck has too many slides for the meeting time or audience | Cut ruthlessly. 15 slides in 45 minutes means 3 minutes per slide -- too shallow. 8 focused slides with discussion time is better |
| **Convert to email** | Customer is not available for a live meeting or the data does not warrant a formal presentation | Send a condensed summary via cc-email-drafter. Not every QBR needs to be a meeting |

## Special Handling

### The Benchmark Conversation

Benchmark data can motivate or demoralise. Know your customer. Some respond to competitive positioning ("your peers do better -- let us help you catch up"). Others find it condescending. Choose which benchmarks to show based on the customer's personality and what will drive the right behaviour.

### Executive Audiences

If the QBR includes C-suite attendees, lead with the financial impact and strategic alignment. They do not care about feature adoption percentages -- they care about ROI, business outcomes, and whether this investment is delivering against their priorities. Restructure the deck accordingly.

### The Strategic Recommendation Slides

The agent marks these as placeholders for good reason. This is where you demonstrate strategic partnership, not vendor status. Your recommendation should be specific to this customer, grounded in their data, and connected to their business goals. Generic advice ("drive more adoption") signals that you have not done the work.
