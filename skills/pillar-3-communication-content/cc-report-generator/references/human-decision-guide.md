# Human Decision Guide: Report Review and Distribution

## Your Role

The Report Generator aggregates data from all pillars into formatted reports for different audiences -- weekly CSM summaries, monthly manager reports, quarterly leadership reviews. Your job is to add the narrative layer, validate that the numbers match reality, and decide what emphasis the audience needs. The agent compiles the data. You provide the insight.

## When You Will See This

Reports are generated on recurring schedules:

1. **Weekly** -- your portfolio summary with priorities, risk queue, and compliance metrics
2. **Monthly** -- team performance report for your manager
3. **Quarterly** -- leadership report with NRR, churn analysis, and strategic outlook
4. **Ad-hoc** -- on-demand deep dives for specific topics

## Decision Framework

### 1. Validate before distributing

Every report should pass your smell test:
- Do the headline numbers match your qualitative sense of the portfolio? If the report says healthy and you feel troubled, investigate the gap
- Has a single large account skewed an aggregate metric? Portfolio-level averages can hide significant individual account issues
- Are the period-over-period comparisons meaningful? A 10% improvement sounds good until you realise last period was unusually bad

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Distribute as-is** | Numbers are accurate and the report speaks for itself | Send to the intended audience. Save your narrative for the discussion |
| **Add commentary** | Numbers are accurate but need context to be interpreted correctly | Add a 3-5 line executive summary explaining the key trends and what they mean |
| **Adjust emphasis** | The default emphasis does not match what the audience needs to hear this period | Restructure. If leadership is focused on competitive churn, lead with that data even if it is not the biggest number |
| **Investigate before distributing** | A number looks wrong or surprising | Do not distribute a report with a number you cannot explain. Investigate first, then distribute with context |
| **Hold** | Report timing conflicts with a sensitive situation (e.g., major churn event still being processed) | Delay until you can present the full picture, not a partial one |

## Special Handling

### The Narrative Gap

Numbers do not explain themselves. "NRR declined 0.3%" is a fact. "NRR declined because two enterprise accounts churned due to competitive displacement in the analytics space" is insight. Leadership values the insight more than the number. Always add the "why."

### CSM Weekly Review

Use your weekly report as a prioritisation tool, not just a status update. The risk queue and overdue items tell you where to spend your time this week. If you are consistently ignoring the report's priorities to fight fires, the issue is capacity -- raise it with your manager.

### Board-Level Summaries

If the report feeds board materials, every number will be scrutinised. Triple-check accuracy. Frame conservatively. A board-level number that is later corrected downward damages credibility far more than a conservative initial estimate that is later revised upward.
