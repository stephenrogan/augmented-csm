# Human Decision Guide: Email Draft Review

## Your Role

The Email Drafter produces personalised, tone-calibrated email drafts for routine customer communications. Your job is to decide whether to send, edit the draft to match the real relationship, and determine when email is not the right channel. The agent writes the words. You own the relationship impact.

## When You Will See This

Email drafts are generated:

1. **Lifecycle-triggered** -- check-in, follow-up, milestone notification, renewal opener
2. **Post-call** -- follow-up email after a customer conversation
3. **On-demand** -- you requested a draft for a specific purpose

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess whether to send

Before reviewing the draft itself:
- Is email the right channel? Some conversations should be a call. Some should wait
- Is the timing right? Friday afternoon emails get buried. Emails during a crisis feel tone-deaf
- Is this customer expecting to hear from you, or will this feel random?

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Send as-is** | Draft is accurate, tone matches the relationship, timing is right | Send. Log the touchpoint |
| **Edit and send** | Draft needs adjustment -- tone is too formal/informal, content needs a personal touch, or data points need removal | Edit to match your voice and the relationship. The draft is a starting point, not a final product |
| **Hold** | Timing is wrong, the customer is in a crisis, or you need more information before reaching out | Park the draft. Set a reminder for when the timing improves |
| **Replace with call** | The topic is too sensitive, complex, or important for email | Discard the draft. Schedule a call instead. Some conversations lose their impact in writing |
| **Suppress** | The email would do more harm than good -- customer is frustrated, over-contacted, or the message has no value | Do not send. A "just checking in" email with nothing to say erodes credibility |

## Special Handling

### At-Risk Account Emails

If the account has active risk signals, scrutinise the draft heavily. A routine check-in email to a customer in crisis is tone-deaf. Either adapt the email to acknowledge the situation or replace it with a direct conversation.

### Tone Calibration

The agent calibrates tone by segment (enterprise = formal, SMB = casual). Your relationship may differ from the segment default. An enterprise customer you have worked with for 2 years may prefer a warmer tone. An SMB contact who is a C-suite executive may prefer more formal. Override the default based on what you know.

### The Nudge Email

When a customer has not responded, the agent generates a shorter, lighter follow-up. Before sending: have you already sent multiple follow-ups? If you are on your third nudge with no response, the problem is not the email copy -- the customer is disengaging. Switch channels or escalate.
