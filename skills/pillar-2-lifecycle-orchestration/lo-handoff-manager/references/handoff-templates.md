# Handoff Templates Reference

Templates for handoff briefs and customer notification emails.

## Sales-to-CS Handoff Brief Template

**Section 1: Deal Summary**
- Account name, ARR, product/tier, contract start date, contract length
- Implementation scope (what was sold, what needs to be set up)
- Expected go-live date

**Section 2: Customer Goals**
- Primary goal: what does the customer expect to achieve?
- Secondary goals: what else was discussed?
- Success metrics: how will the customer measure value?
- Timeline expectations: when does the customer expect results?

**Section 3: Key Contacts**
- Champion: name, role, engagement level, communication preference
- Economic buyer: name, role, involvement in sales process
- Technical lead: name, role (if applicable)
- Other stakeholders: names, roles, influence level

**Section 4: Technical Requirements**
- Integrations needed
- Data migration scope
- Security/compliance requirements
- SSO/authentication setup

**Section 5: Competitive Context**
- Other vendors evaluated (and why customer chose you)
- Known product gaps or concerns raised during evaluation
- Pricing sensitivity or comparison points

**Section 6: Promises and Red Flags**
- Commitments made by sales (feature timelines, custom terms, pricing)
- Known concerns or sensitivities
- Political dynamics (internal champions vs. sceptics)

## CSM-to-CSM Handoff Brief Template

**Section 1: Account Snapshot**
- [Generated from bi-account-brief -- full account context]

**Section 2: Relationship Context (Human Input Required)**
- Relationship strength per contact (strong/developing/weak/strained)
- Communication preferences (formal/informal, email/call, frequency)
- Personal rapport notes (interests, conversation style, hot buttons)
- Trust level (high/moderate/low -- and why)

**Section 3: Current State**
- Where is the account in its lifecycle?
- What is actively in progress? (adoption plans, support issues, pending actions)
- What was planned for the next 90 days?

**Section 4: History Highlights**
- Key wins: moments that built trust and demonstrated value
- Key crises: incidents that damaged trust (and how they were resolved)
- Strategic pivots: changes in the customer's goals or your approach

**Section 5: Landmines**
- Topics to avoid or handle carefully
- Known frustrations or unresolved concerns
- Internal political dynamics the new CSM should navigate

**Section 6: Recommendations**
- First 7-day plan: what the new CSM should do immediately
- 30-day priorities: what to focus on in the first month
- Ongoing approach: cadence, style, strategic priorities

## Customer Transition Notification Email

**From**: Outgoing CSM (or joint send)
**To**: Primary customer contact
**CC**: New CSM

**Structure**:
1. Personal note from outgoing CSM: gratitude for the partnership, confidence in the transition
2. Introduction of new CSM: name, brief background, why they are a good fit
3. What will not change: service level, commitments, ongoing support
4. Next step: new CSM will reach out within [X days] to introduce themselves
5. Warm sign-off

**Tone**: Warm, confident, reassuring. The customer should feel valued, not abandoned. Never frame the transition as a disruption.
