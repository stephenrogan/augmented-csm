# Human Decision Guide: Account Handoff Execution

## Your Role

The Handoff Manager coordinates the mechanics of account transitions -- sales-to-CS, CSM-to-CSM, or segment reassignment. Your job is to ensure the relationship survives the transition. The agent moves data and schedules events. You transfer context, trust, and momentum.

## When You Will See This

Handoffs are triggered when:

1. **New customer** -- sales closes a deal and the account transitions to CS
2. **CSM reassignment** -- account moves between CSMs (territory change, capacity rebalance, departure)
3. **Segment migration** -- account graduates from SMB to mid-market or mid-market to enterprise

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess transition risk

Not all handoffs carry equal risk. Before proceeding:
- Is the account healthy or at risk? At-risk accounts should not be handed off without a stabilisation plan
- Is the customer relationship single-threaded? Single-threaded accounts are highest risk during transitions
- Is a renewal within 90 days? If so, consider delaying the handoff or co-managing through renewal

### 2. Decide on approach

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Standard handoff** | Healthy account, multiple contacts, no imminent milestone | Follow the handoff checklist. Warm introduction, context transfer, 30-day overlap |
| **High-risk handoff** | At-risk account, single-threaded, or near renewal | Extend overlap period. Co-attend meetings. Incoming CSM should not fly solo until relationship is established |
| **Emergency handoff** | CSM departure with no advance notice | Prioritise by ARR and renewal proximity. Manager covers critical accounts until reassignment. Speed matters more than process |
| **Sales-to-CS handoff** | New customer closing | Attend the final sales call or kickoff. Ensure sales shares deal context, customer expectations, and any promises made during the sales process |
| **Delay the handoff** | Active escalation, renewal in progress, or critical save play underway | Complete the current motion first. Changing CSMs mid-crisis compounds the problem |

## Special Handling

### Promises Made During Sales

The single biggest source of post-handoff friction. During sales-to-CS handoffs, explicitly ask: "What was committed during the sales process?" If promises were made that CS cannot deliver, surface this immediately -- not after the customer raises it.

### Knowledge That Lives in the CSM's Head

The handoff brief captures system data. It cannot capture: informal commitments, personality preferences, internal politics, unwritten expectations, or the customer's real priorities versus their stated ones. A 30-minute live debrief between outgoing and incoming CSM is non-negotiable for any account above SMB.

### Customer Communication

The customer should hear about the transition from a human, not discover it when a new name appears on their next meeting invite. The outgoing CSM introduces the incoming CSM with context and confidence. The customer should feel like an upgrade, not a disruption.
