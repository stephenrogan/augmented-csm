# Onboarding Templates

Templates for all communications and documents generated during the onboarding workflow.

## Email Templates

### Welcome Email

**Trigger**: Handoff brief approved by CSM
**Personalisation fields**: {customer_name}, {primary_contact_first_name}, {csm_name}, {company_name}, {product_tier}, {primary_use_case}

**Structure**:
1. Introduction of the CSM (1-2 sentences)
2. Confirmation of what was agreed during the sales process (pulled from handoff brief)
3. What happens next (kickoff call, timeline expectations)
4. One clear action item for the customer (e.g., "Please confirm availability for a kickoff call next week")

**Tone**: Warm, confident, action-oriented. Not overly formal. The customer just bought -- this is the start of the relationship, not a legal document.

### Day 7 Check-In

**Trigger**: 7 days from kickoff
**Personalisation fields**: {primary_contact_first_name}, {configuration_status}, {csm_name}

**Structure**:
1. Brief check-in (1 sentence)
2. Acknowledge progress if configuration milestones have been hit
3. Offer help if configuration is incomplete
4. One question: "Is there anything blocking your setup?"

### Adoption Tips Sequence (3 emails)

**Email 1 -- Getting Started** (Day 1 post-first-login):
- Confirm they are set up
- 3 quick wins they can achieve today
- Link to relevant help centre resources

**Email 2 -- Going Deeper** (Day 7 post-first-login):
- Introduce one advanced feature relevant to their use case
- One customer example of value achieved
- Prompt: "Have you tried [specific feature]?"

**Email 3 -- Measuring Impact** (Day 14 post-first-login):
- Prompt them to reflect on outcomes so far
- Introduce the idea of a check-in to review progress
- Tease what comes next (QBR, expanded use cases)

### Onboarding Completion Email

**Trigger**: Lifecycle stage changed to "Adopted"
**Structure**:
1. Congratulate on successful onboarding
2. Summary of what was achieved (usage milestones, setup completed)
3. Introduce the ongoing support model (check-in cadence, how to get help)
4. Set expectation for next touchpoint

## Deck Templates

### Kickoff Deck

**Slides**:
1. Title: Welcome to [product] -- [customer name] Kickoff
2. Agenda
3. Your goals (pulled from sales handoff -- what success looks like)
4. Onboarding timeline (phase overview with dates)
5. Your team (CSM intro, support contacts, escalation path)
6. What we need from you (customer responsibilities, access requirements)
7. Next steps and action items

### 30-Day Progress Report

**Sections**:
1. Onboarding timeline: planned vs. actual
2. Milestones: completed, in progress, at risk
3. Usage metrics: current adoption snapshot
4. Open items: blockers, pending actions
5. Recommendations: CSM's assessment of next priorities

# Segment Variations

How the onboarding workflow adapts based on customer segment.

## Enterprise (>EUR 100k ARR)

- **Duration**: 45-90 days (longer due to complexity)
- **Kickoff**: Formal, multi-stakeholder. May include executive sponsor from both sides
- **Configuration**: Often involves custom integrations, SSO setup, data migration. Technical resources from both sides
- **Touchpoint frequency**: Weekly during Phase 2, bi-weekly during Phase 3
- **First-value milestone**: Typically defined as a specific business outcome, not just feature adoption
- **Customisation**: High. Templates are starting points; CSM customises heavily

## Mid-Market (EUR 20k-100k ARR)

- **Duration**: 30-45 days
- **Kickoff**: Standard, typically with 2-3 customer contacts
- **Configuration**: Standard setup with moderate customisation. Customer handles most configuration with guidance
- **Touchpoint frequency**: Check-ins at Day 7, Day 14, Day 30
- **First-value milestone**: Core use case operational and delivering measurable output
- **Customisation**: Moderate. Templates work with light personalisation

## SMB (<EUR 20k ARR)

- **Duration**: 14-21 days
- **Kickoff**: May be replaced by a self-serve onboarding flow with an optional "office hours" slot
- **Configuration**: Self-serve with automated guidance. No dedicated CSM involvement unless flagged
- **Touchpoint frequency**: Automated email sequence only unless the customer requests help
- **First-value milestone**: First core workflow completed
- **Customisation**: Minimal. Fully templated. Scalability over personalisation
