# Renewal Playbook Reference

Detailed renewal workflow by risk classification.

## On Track Renewals

**Profile**: Health > 75, no active risk signals, engagement stable or growing.

**Approach**: Standard process. The renewal should be a formality, not a negotiation.

**Timeline**:
- T-120: Confirm renewal on CSM's radar. No special action needed
- T-90: Generate renewal brief. Confirm economic buyer is engaged. Begin value narrative preparation
- T-60: CSM raises renewal in regular check-in. Confirms intent to renew. Identifies any expansion opportunity
- T-30: Renewal paperwork in progress. Track CRM stage
- T-0: Renewed

**CSM time investment**: Low. 2-3 hours total across the 6-month window.

## Watch Renewals

**Profile**: Health 65-75, or 1 active risk signal, or engagement flat.

**Approach**: Proactive engagement. The renewal is likely but not certain. Address the watch factor before renewal conversation.

**Timeline**:
- T-120: Identify the watch factor. Is it health-driven, engagement-driven, or signal-driven? Build a plan to address before T-90
- T-90: Assess whether the watch factor has improved. If yes, proceed as On Track. If no, increase engagement
- T-60: CSM has a focused conversation about the customer's satisfaction and future plans. Listen for concerns
- T-30: If renewal is proceeding, track as normal. If concerns remain, escalate to manager for strategy review
- T-0: Renewed (with or without adjustments)

**CSM time investment**: Moderate. 5-8 hours total. Most invested in understanding and addressing the watch factor.

## At Risk Renewals

**Profile**: Health < 65, or 2+ risk signals, or champion departure, or competitive signal.

**Approach**: Active intervention. The renewal is not a given. The CSM must understand and address the risk before asking for the renewal.

**Timeline**:
- T-120: Full risk assessment. Pull account brief, review all signals, identify root cause. CSM + manager strategy session
- T-90: Execute risk mitigation plan. This may include: executive engagement, product fix escalation, additional support resources, relationship recovery
- T-60: Assess mitigation progress. Has the risk reduced? If yes, proceed with renewal conversation. If no, escalate further or prepare concession strategy
- T-30: Renewal conversation with full awareness of risk factors. May require pricing adjustment, extended terms, or additional commitments to secure renewal
- T-0: Renewed (with concessions or adjustments) or churned

**CSM time investment**: High. 15-25 hours total. This is the most important work a CSM does.

## Critical Renewals

**Profile**: Health < 50, or competitive signal + renewal < 90 days, or customer has stated intent to leave.

**Approach**: Full save play. Executive engagement. This is a potential churn event that requires organisational response, not just CSM effort.

**Timeline**:
- Immediate: CSM briefs manager and CS leadership. Full save team assembled
- T-90 or immediately if closer: Executive-to-executive engagement. Address the root cause at the highest level
- Ongoing: Dedicated resources to resolve the issue. Product escalation, commercial flexibility, relationship recovery
- T-30: Decision point -- is the save working? If yes, secure renewal with whatever terms are needed. If no, begin graceful offboarding and knowledge capture

**CSM time investment**: Very high. 30+ hours. This account dominates the CSM's portfolio for the duration.
