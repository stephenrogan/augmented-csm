# Human Decision Guide: Renewal Management

## Your Role

The Renewal Manager tracks timelines, triggers milestone workflows, and surfaces readiness signals. Your job is to own the commercial outcome: deciding the renewal strategy, leading the conversation, navigating objections, and securing the renewal. The agent manages the clock. You manage the deal.

## When You Will See This

Renewal alerts surface at defined milestones:

1. **T-180** -- initial renewal flag with health and contract data
2. **T-120** -- renewal risk assessment with classification
3. **T-90** -- active renewal phase begins with prep brief
4. **T-60** -- renewal conversation expected, flag if no activity
5. **T-30** -- escalation check if renewal not in progress
6. **T-14** -- final check before renewal date

## Decision Framework

### 1. Validate the risk classification

The agent classifies renewal risk based on health, signals, and engagement. Before accepting:
- Does the classification match your relationship read? You may know the customer is committed despite low health scores, or at risk despite good data
- Are there factors the data cannot see? Budget changes, competitive conversations, or internal politics

### 2. Decide on approach

| Classification | When to Use | Next Step |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| **On Track** | Health strong, engagement active, no risk signals | Standard renewal process. Focus on value narrative and any expansion opportunity. Do not over-manage |
| **Watch** | One or two soft signals but relationship is solid | Increase touchpoint frequency. Prepare a value narrative. Address the signals proactively before they compound |
| **At Risk** | Multiple signals, declining health, or stakeholder gaps | Immediate strategy session with your manager. Build a save plan. Address root causes, not symptoms |
| **Critical** | Customer has expressed intent to leave, or competitive displacement is active | Executive engagement. Full save play. Every day matters. Brief leadership on the situation and your plan |

## Special Handling

### Renewal Plus Expansion

If the account is healthy with expansion signals, combine the conversations carefully. Lead with value delivered, then transition to growth. Never let the renewal feel transactional -- it should feel like a natural continuation of a successful partnership.

### The Silent Renewal

If you reach T-60 with no renewal conversation started and the customer is not responding to outreach, this is a critical signal. Silence near renewal is not neutral -- it usually means the decision is being made without you. Escalate immediately.

### Pricing Objections

When the customer pushes on price, lead with value, not discounts. Use pa-value-reporter evidence to quantify ROI. If a discount is warranted, tie it to a commitment (multi-year, expanded scope, reference participation). Never discount reactively without understanding the real objection.

### Auto-Renewal Accounts

Even with auto-renewal clauses, do not assume the renewal is safe. Customers can still churn at the next available window. Use auto-renewal accounts as an opportunity to run a lighter version of the renewal process -- value conversation without the commercial pressure.
