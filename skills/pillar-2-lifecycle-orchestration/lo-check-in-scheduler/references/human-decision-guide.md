# Human Decision Guide: Check-In Cadence Management

## Your Role

The Check-In Scheduler maintains touchpoint cadence across your portfolio based on segment, health, and lifecycle stage. Your job is to decide when to override the default cadence, what to discuss in each check-in, and when a scheduled touchpoint is not the right approach. The agent manages the calendar. You manage the conversation.

## When You Will See This

The scheduler surfaces accounts to you when:

1. **Touchpoint is due** -- a cadence-driven check-in is scheduled
2. **Cadence override recommended** -- health or risk signals suggest increasing or decreasing frequency
3. **Compliance gap** -- you have missed expected touchpoints on one or more accounts

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess whether the touchpoint is needed

Not every scheduled check-in should happen. Before confirming:
- Did you engage this account through another channel this week? If so, the cadence requirement may already be met
- Is the account in a stable state with no open items? A check-in with nothing to discuss erodes the customer's time and your credibility
- Is there a better format? Some "check-ins" should be a quick email, not a 30-minute call

### 2. Decide on approach

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Proceed with check-in** | Account has open items, upcoming milestones, or enough time has passed that re-engagement is valuable | Prepare with account brief. Have a specific topic or question -- never "just checking in" |
| **Replace with async** | No open items but cadence requires a touchpoint | Send a personalised email via cc-email-drafter with a relevant observation or data point |
| **Increase cadence** | Health declining, risk signal active, renewal approaching, or onboarding in progress | Override the default. Set a tighter cadence until the situation stabilises |
| **Decrease cadence** | Stable, healthy, low-touch account receiving too many touchpoints | Reduce cadence. Over-touching healthy accounts wastes your capacity and annoys the customer |
| **Skip this cycle** | You engaged meaningfully within the last few days through another channel | Mark as covered. Log the alternative touchpoint so engagement tracking remains accurate |

## Special Handling

### At-Risk Accounts

When a risk signal is active, the scheduler may recommend increased cadence. Before adding more meetings, consider whether more frequent touchpoints will help or create fatigue. Sometimes one well-prepared call is better than three rushed check-ins.

### Post-Escalation

After an escalation is resolved, do not immediately revert to normal cadence. Schedule a recovery check-in 7-14 days post-resolution to confirm the fix held and the relationship has stabilised.

### Compliance Gaps

If you are consistently behind on cadence across multiple accounts, the issue is capacity, not discipline. Raise it with your manager. A compliance gap spread across the portfolio means something needs to change -- account load, segment mix, or support model.
