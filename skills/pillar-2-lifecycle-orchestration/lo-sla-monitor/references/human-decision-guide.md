# Human Decision Guide: Commitment and SLA Triage

## Your Role

The SLA Monitor tracks every commitment made to customers -- contractual SLAs, action items from calls, and informal promises. Your job is to ensure follow-through, manage breaches when they occur, and prevent a pattern of broken commitments from eroding trust. The agent watches the clock. You protect the relationship.

## When You Will See This

SLA alerts surface when:

1. **Commitment due soon** -- a deadline is within 3 business days
2. **Commitment at risk** -- deadline within 1 day with no evidence of completion
3. **Commitment breached** -- deadline passed without delivery
4. **Pattern alert** -- an account has multiple overdue commitments or a declining completion rate

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess the breach impact

Not all breaches are equal. Before deciding:
- How visible is this commitment to the customer? A missed internal deadline is different from a missed customer-facing promise
- How many times has this customer experienced a breach? First-time breaches are forgivable. Patterns destroy trust
- What is the relationship context? A breach during a save play or near renewal is far more damaging than one on a stable account

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Deliver now** | Commitment is at risk but still deliverable before deadline | Drop everything and deliver. A last-minute delivery is infinitely better than a miss |
| **Renegotiate** | Delivery is not possible by the deadline but the customer has not yet noticed | Contact the customer proactively. Explain the delay, give a new date, and deliver on the new date without fail |
| **Recover** | Commitment was breached and the customer knows | Acknowledge the miss directly. Do not make excuses. Provide the deliverable immediately with a concrete plan to prevent recurrence |
| **Escalate internally** | The commitment depends on another team that is not delivering | Route through ic-escalation-router with specific ask and deadline. Your name is on the commitment even if someone else owns the work |
| **Absorb** | Minor commitment, customer has not noticed, and drawing attention to it would do more harm than good | Deliver quietly and immediately. Log the miss internally for your own accountability tracking |

## Special Handling

### Commitment Patterns

If an account's completion rate drops below 80%, the issue is systemic, not incidental. Review the types of commitments being missed. Are they unrealistic promises? Internal team failures? Your own workload overflow? Address the root cause, not just the individual breaches.

### Commitments Made by Other Teams

When engineering, product, or support makes a commitment to your customer, you still own the follow-through from the customer's perspective. They do not care about internal handoffs. Track these commitments as aggressively as your own.

### The "I'll Look Into That" Problem

Vague commitments with no deadline are the most dangerous. They feel like promises to the customer but have no accountability mechanism. When you catch yourself saying "I'll look into that," immediately define what you will do and by when.
