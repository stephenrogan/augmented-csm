# Human Decision Guide: QBR Orchestration

## Your Role

The QBR Orchestrator manages the logistics and timeline -- scheduling, content triggers, stakeholder coordination, and follow-up tracking. Your job is to make the QBR strategically valuable: deciding what story to tell, which data to highlight, what recommendations to make, and how to use the meeting to deepen the relationship. The agent runs the process. You run the room.

## When You Will See This

QBR orchestration alerts surface when:

1. **QBR due** -- an account's QBR cadence indicates scheduling is needed
2. **Content ready** -- the QBR deck has been generated and needs your review
3. **Scheduling stalled** -- the customer has not responded to scheduling attempts
4. **Post-QBR follow-up due** -- action items from the QBR need to be executed

## Decision Framework

### 1. Decide whether a QBR is the right format

Not every account needs a formal QBR. Before scheduling:
- Is there enough to discuss? A QBR with no meaningful content wastes the customer's time
- Is the customer's stakeholder landscape right for a QBR? You need the right people in the room
- Would a different format serve better? Some accounts benefit more from an informal strategy conversation than a slide deck review

### 2. Decide on approach

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Full QBR** | Strategic account, executive audience, enough data and narrative for a meaningful review | Customise the deck from cc-qbr-deck-builder. Write the strategic recommendation slides. Prepare discussion questions |
| **Lightweight review** | Mid-market account or one with limited data changes since last QBR | Condense to 5-6 slides. Focus on value delivered and one forward-looking recommendation |
| **Skip this cycle** | Account is too early (just onboarded), too small (ROI of a QBR does not justify the prep), or customer has declined | Document the skip. Ensure value is communicated through other channels |
| **Rescue QBR** | Account is at risk and the QBR is a chance to reset the narrative | Reframe the QBR around the recovery plan. Lead with acknowledgement of challenges, then present the path forward |
| **Expansion QBR** | Account is healthy with expansion signals | Use the QBR to plant the expansion seed. Lead with value, then naturally transition to growth opportunities |

## Special Handling

### The Strategic Recommendations Slide

This is the most valuable slide in the deck -- and the one the agent cannot write. Your recommendation should be specific, evidence-backed, and demonstrate that you understand the customer's business. "Continue driving adoption" is worthless. "Expand API integration to the analytics team to reduce the 4 hours per week they spend on manual reporting" is actionable.

### Scheduling Difficulties

If the customer resists scheduling, do not keep pushing. Ask why. They may not see value in QBRs (your problem to fix), may be too busy (adjust format), or may be disengaging (bigger problem). Persistent scheduling resistance is an engagement signal.

### Post-QBR Momentum

The QBR is worthless if nothing happens after it. Within 48 hours: send the follow-up email with captured action items, update the success plan, and set deadlines. The post-QBR follow-through is where trust is built or lost.
