# Human Decision Guide: Milestone Progress Review

## Your Role

The Milestone Tracker monitors success plan milestones, flags overdue items, and tracks progress against timelines. Your job is to interpret delays, decide when a missed milestone matters, and adjust plans when reality diverges from the original timeline. The agent tracks dates. You manage outcomes.

## When You Will See This

Milestone alerts surface when:

1. **Milestone approaching** -- a success plan milestone is due within 7 days
2. **Milestone overdue** -- a milestone has passed its target date without completion
3. **Milestone achieved early** -- an account hit a target ahead of schedule (expansion signal)
4. **Plan stalled** -- no milestone progress for 30+ days

## Decision Framework

### 1. Assess the delay

Not every overdue milestone is a problem. Before escalating:
- Was the original timeline realistic? Many success plans are over-optimistic at creation
- Is the delay customer-driven or internal? Customer-driven delays (their team is busy, priorities shifted) require a different response than internal ones (your team dropped the ball)
- Does this milestone gate subsequent milestones? A delay on a non-critical path milestone is less urgent than one that blocks everything downstream

### 2. Decide on action

| Situation | When to Use | Next Step |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Reschedule** | Delay is minor, cause is understood, no downstream impact | Update the timeline. Communicate the new date to the customer. No drama needed |
| **Investigate** | Milestone is overdue and cause is unclear | Reach out to the customer contact responsible. Do not assume -- ask what is blocking progress |
| **Escalate internally** | Delay is caused by an internal team (engineering, product, support) not delivering | Route through ic-escalation-router with specific ask and deadline |
| **Adjust the plan** | Multiple milestones are slipping, indicating the plan itself is unrealistic | Rework the success plan with the customer. Acknowledge the reality and rebuild credibility |
| **Celebrate** | Milestone achieved early or on time | Recognise the achievement with the customer. Early milestones are evidence for value conversations and QBRs |

## Special Handling

### Onboarding Milestones

Delays during onboarding compound rapidly. Every week of delayed activation is a week the customer is not getting value from their investment. If onboarding milestones slip by more than 2 weeks, escalate to your manager and consider whether the onboarding approach needs adjustment.

### Pre-Renewal Milestones

Success plan milestones that are overdue when renewal approaches undermine the renewal conversation. You cannot credibly say "look at all the value we delivered" if the plan is half-complete. Prioritise overdue milestones on accounts renewing within 90 days.

### Customer-Driven Stalls

When the customer is the bottleneck (they have not provided data, assigned resources, or completed their tasks), document the dependency clearly. You cannot force action, but you can ensure the delay is visible and attributed correctly -- both for your manager and for the customer's internal stakeholders.
